Summary Measures Worksheet: Exercise 7.2

The task was to determine whether the mean income of males and females were significantly different from the data set provide

Since the calculated F value is less than the critical value, it can be assumed that there is no significant difference between the variances for the two variables. Hence we use a two tailed t test with equal variances to answer the question: "Does the mean income for males exceed that of females?"

To do this we test a null hypotheis that:

There is no difference between the population mean income for males and females.

The results are shown below.

Interpretation

Since the calculated value of t (3.268) is less than the Critical value of t (1.98) we reject the null hypothesis since the probabilty of this difference being purely due to chance is less than 1% (as p=0.001).

Assumptions

We have already assumed there is no significant differences in the variances using the F test and hence the appropriate type of t test chosen.

Since the t test is a parametric test, assumptions here are that the income data is normally distributed. This could be confirmed by plotting the data to produce a distribution curve / histogram and confirmed with another tests such as Shapiro-Wilk (since number of observations is less than 100)

The Case

Ricardo works for the records department of his local government as a computer records clerk, where he has access to files of property tax records. For a scientific study, a researcher, Beth, has been granted access to the numerical portion “but not the corresponding names” of some records. Beth finds some information that she would like to use, but she needs the names and addresses corresponding with certain properties. Beth asks Ricardo to retrieve these names and addresses, so she can contact these people for more information and for permission to do further study. Now consider, what are the ethical issues involved in deciding which of these options to pursue?

  • If Ricardo is not responsible for determining allowable access, should he release the names and addresses?
  • Suppose Ricardo were responsible for determining allowable access to the files. What ethical issues would be involved in his deciding whether to grant access to Beth?
  • Should Beth be allowed to contact the individuals involved? That is, should the Records department release individuals' names to a researcher? What are the ethical issues for the Records department to consider?
  • Suppose Beth contacts the individuals to ask their permission, and one-third of them respond giving permission, one-third respond denying permission, and one-third do not respond. Beth claims that at least one-half of the individuals are needed to make a valid study. What options are available to Beth?

    • Be prepared to discuss your answers during the seminar session.

    My Response

    Ricardo's Responsibility: Ricardo's role as a records clerk grants him access to sensitive information, raising concerns about confidentiality and data protection. Even if Ricardo is not responsible for determining access, he still holds a duty to safeguard individuals' privacy rights and ensure compliance with organisational policies and regulations regarding data access and sharing.

    Granting Access: If Ricardo were responsible for determining access, ethical considerations would revolve around balancing the researcher's need for information with the potential risks to individuals' privacy. He would need to assess the researcher's intentions, the necessity of the requested data, and the potential impact on individuals' rights and well-being before deciding. Moreover, Ricardo would need to consider the implications of cross-border privacy laws, as data protection regulations may vary across different regions, complicating the decision-making process further.

    Contacting Individuals: Releasing individuals' names to a researcher raises ethical concerns about informed consent and potential harm. The Records department must weigh the researcher's objectives against individuals' rights to privacy and autonomy. Any contact with individuals should be conducted ethically, ensuring that they are fully informed about the purpose of the study and could consent or decline participation without coercion.

    Validity of Beth's Study: Beth's study validity hinges on obtaining sufficient participant consent. However, her reliance on a specific response rate for validity raises ethical questions about the threshold for informed consent and the ethical conduct of research. Beth should explore alternative methods for obtaining consent or consider revising her study design to ensure ethical integrity and participant well-being.

Authors: Wejdan Aljohani et al.

Publication: Aljohani, Wejdan & Mohamed, Nazar. (2020). Measuring Cyber Security Awareness of Students: A Case Study at Fahad Bin Sultan University. 9. 141-155.

Paper 2. Title: Cyber Risk and Cybersecurity: A Systematic Review of Data Availability

Authors: Frank Cremer, Barry Sheehan, Michael Fortmann, Arash N. Kia, Martin Mullins, Finbarr Murphy, and Stefan Materne

Publication: Cremer F, Sheehan B, Fortmann M, Kia AN, Mullins M, Murphy F, Materne S. Cyber risk and cybersecurity: a systematic review of data availability. Geneva Pap Risk Insur Issues Pract. 2022;47(3):698-736. doi: 10.1057/s41288-022-00266-6. Epub 2022 Feb 17. PMID: 35194352; PMCID: PMC8853293.

Familiarisation with Purpose, Problem, Objective, or Research Question

Paper 1: Cyber Security Awareness Among University Students

Purpose: To measure the level of cybersecurity awareness among university students.

Problem: The increasing use of digital devices and the Internet has heightened cybersecurity vulnerabilities, making it essential for university students to be aware of these risks.

Objective: To evaluate students' knowledge of cybersecurity and recommend measures to improve awareness.

Research Questions:

  • How much do students know about information security?
  • What can be done to ensure students remain safe in online communities?

Contribution to Knowledge: Provides insights into the cybersecurity awareness levels of university students and highlights the need for educational interventions.

Paper 2: Cyber Risk and Cybersecurity: A Systematic Review of Data Availability

Purpose: To systematically review the availability of data related to cyber risks and cybersecurity.

Problem: The continuous development of cyber risks and the need for robust countermeasures require comprehensive data availability for effective analysis.

Objective: To assess the current state of data availability for cybersecurity research.

Methodology: Systematic review based on the PRISMA framework, focusing on peer-reviewed articles published in the last 10 years that use cybersecurity or cyber risk datasets.

Contribution to Knowledge: Offers a detailed overview of existing datasets in the field of cybersecurity, aiding future research by highlighting gaps and available resources.

Data Collection and Analysis

Paper 1: Cyber Security Awareness Among University Students

Data Collection: Questionnaire administered to university students.

Analysis: Statistical analysis of questionnaire responses to determine awareness levels.

Appropriateness: The use of questionnaires and statistical analysis is appropriate for quantitatively assessing awareness levels.

Justification: These methods allow for objective measurement and analysis of students' cybersecurity awareness.

Limitations: Potential biases may exist in questionnaire responses, such as social desirability bias. Additionally, the study's findings may be limited to the specific university context.

Paper 2: Cyber Risk and Cybersecurity: A Systematic Review of Data Availability

Data Collection: Literature search across multiple databases (IEEE Xplore, Scopus, SpringerLink, Web of Science).

Analysis: Systematic review of articles using the PRISMA framework.

Appropriateness: The comprehensive search and systematic review process ensure a thorough examination of available data.

Justification: This method is effective for identifying and summarising the state of data availability in cybersecurity research.

Limitations: Limitations may include publication bias, as only peer-reviewed articles were included, and the possibility of missing relevant articles despite comprehensive search strategies.

Suggestions for Paper Improvements

  1. Clearer Research Questions: While the research questions provided are good, they could be more specific to guide the research process more effectively. For example, adding details such as "What specific cyber threats are university students most aware of?" and "What educational interventions have proven effective in enhancing cybersecurity awareness among university students?" can add depth to the inquiry.

  2. Clarity in Methodology Description: In Paper 2, it's mentioned that the methodology is based on the PRISMA framework, which is great for systematic reviews. However, it might be helpful to briefly explain what the PRISMA framework entails for readers who may not be familiar with it. This could provide context and transparency regarding the systematic review process.

  3. Expansion of Data Collection Details: While both papers briefly mention their data collection methods, providing more detail on the questionnaire used in Paper 1 and the criteria for article selection in Paper 2 would enhance transparency and reproducibility.

  4. Discussion of Limitations: Including a brief discussion of the limitations of each study would add depth to the papers. For example, potential biases in questionnaire responses in Paper 1 or limitations in the search strategy in Paper 2 could be acknowledged and discussed.

  5. Visual Aids: Consider incorporating visual aids such as charts, graphs, or tables to illustrate key findings, particularly in the Data Collection and Analysis sections. Visual representations can enhance understanding and engagement with the material.

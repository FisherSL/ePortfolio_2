Literature Review Plan

Smart Grids: an example of IoT cyber security challenges for critical infrastructure.

1. Introduction (400 words)

  • What is critical infrastructure with definition & examples.
  • Why critical infrastructure is an attractive target for cyber attacks with examples of potential disruption that could be caused. Use statistics for number of attacks plus give some specific examples of successful breaches and their consequences.
  • Definition, prevalence & importance of IoT with relevant statistics.
  • High level overview of why IoT devices are of particular concern from a cybersecurity point of view e.g. resource constraints, legacy systems (SCADA), insecure communication protocols plus others? tbc.
  • Application of IoT in power generation & distribution (Smart Grids). Brief outline of their advantages compared with traditional power grids.
  • Why is this review useful?
  • Statement describing the structure of the review.

Question: Do I need a methodology section here for how I identified relevant literature to include in the review?

2. IoT Smart Grid Architecture. (250 words)

  • Smart grid architecture based on NIST conceptual model (compare with traditional architecture??) Diagram(s) will be useful.
  • Definition and roles of sensors, actuators, and communication.
  • Concept of real time monitoring & control of sensors and actuators plus data driven intelligent power generation and distribution.
  • Four-layer model detail.

3. Main cybersecurity threats to smart grids. (1200 words?)

  • Discussion of the need for categorisation models for attack types on smart grids
  • Description and possible consequences of each type of attack to include real word examples, if available to include false data injection, man in the middle, replay, denial of service & brute forcing.
  • Include the importance of considering supply chain attacks.

5. Conclusion
Summarise main points.

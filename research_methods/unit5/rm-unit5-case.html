Case Study: Inappropriate Use of Surveys

In 2018, Cambridge Analytica was in the news in the United Kingdom and the USA (Confessore, 2018) for obtaining and sharing data obtained from millions of Facebook users. They obtained the data through innocuous surveys on Facebook (you may have seen this type of survey and probably participated at times). This is probably the highest profile of surveys used for alternative means and, probably, monetary gains. However, this happens often through various media.

Consider how exactly this happened and why it was used. Find one or two further examples of inappropriate use of surveys and highlight the impact of all these examples from the various ethical, social, legal, and professional standpoints that apply.

Record your findings in your e-Portfolio. You can also submit your findings to your tutor for formative feedback.

The Case of Cambridge Analytica

The case of Cambridge Analytica's inappropriate use of surveys serves as a prominent example of how surveys can be misused for unethical purposes. Here's a breakdown of how it happened and its impacts:

  1. Data Obtained Through Innocuous Surveys: Cambridge Analytica obtained data from millions of Facebook users by using innocuous surveys. Users were often enticed to participate in these surveys without fully understanding the implications of sharing their personal information (Boldyreva, 2018).
  2. Unethical Data Sharing: The data collected through these surveys were then used for purposes beyond what was disclosed to the participants. Cambridge Analytica allegedly used this data to create psychographic profiles of individuals for political targeting and manipulation purposes, without obtaining proper consent or informing users about how their data would be used (Boldyreva, 2018).
  3. Impact:
    • Ethical Impact: The unethical collection and use of personal data raise serious ethical concerns about privacy, consent, and transparency. Users' trust in online platforms and survey methodologies can be significantly eroded (Wilson, 2019).
    • Social Impact: The manipulation of individuals' data for political purposes can undermine democratic processes and lead to social polarisation (Hinds et al., 2020; Heawood, 2018). It can also contribute to the spread of misinformation and the amplification of divisive narratives (van der Linden, 2023).
    • Legal Impact: The unauthorised access and misuse of personal data may violate data protection laws and regulations, leading to legal repercussions for the responsible parties.
    • Professional Impact: The scandal highlights the importance of ethical conduct and professional responsibility in data collection and analysis practices. It underscores the need for researchers and organisations to adhere to ethical guidelines and standards when conducting surveys and handling sensitive data.

Further Examples of Inappropriate Use of Surveys

  1. Online Scams: Some individuals or organisations may use surveys as a guise to collect personal information for fraudulent purposes, such as identity theft or financial scams. For example, phishing surveys disguised as legitimate market research studies may trick participants into revealing sensitive information.
  2. Employment Discrimination: Surveys administered by employers or hiring agencies may be used to gather discriminatory information about job applicants, such as age, race, gender, or disability status. This information could then be used to make biased hiring decisions, leading to discrimination and inequality in the workplace.

In conclusion, the inappropriate use of surveys, as exemplified by the Cambridge Analytica scandal and other instances, has far-reaching ethical, social, legal, and professional implications. It underscores the importance of safeguarding individuals' privacy rights, promoting transparency and accountability in data collection practices, and upholding ethical standards in research and data analysis.

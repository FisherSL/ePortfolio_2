Reflecting on Unit 9

In Unit 9, we focused on understanding the critical concepts of validity, generalisability, and reliability in research. These concepts are essential for ensuring the credibility and applicability of research findings. The unit emphasised the importance of considering these factors before collecting any data to ensure accurate and meaningful results.

The unit explored the differences between qualitative and quantitative data and highlighted the importance of planning data analysis and presentation methods beforehand. Quantitative data often leads to descriptive and inferential statistics, while qualitative data requires thorough data cleansing and validation processes.

Reliability refers to the consistency of the measurement process, while validity ensures that the measurement targets the correct concept. A reliable measurement process is a prerequisite for assessing validity, which is a broader and more complex concept.

Key Takeaways:

  1. Validity and Reliability: Understanding the importance of ensuring both reliability and validity in research to draw accurate conclusions.
  2. Generalisability: Recognising how findings from a sample can be applicable to a larger population.
  3. Data Analysis Planning: The necessity of planning data analysis and presentation methods before data collection.

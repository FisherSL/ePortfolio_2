In this section of my ePortfolio, I will explore the foundational concepts and practical applications of research methods. The aim is to develop a solid understanding of the tools and techniques necessary for conducting research effectively in real-world settings. Throughout this module, I will delve into various research strategies and methodologies, enabling me to critically evaluate existing literature and formulate robust research proposals. Additionally, I will examine the ethical, legal, and professional considerations relevant to computing projects, fostering a reflective and independent approach to my learning process. By the end of this section, I expect to have acquired the ability to apply key research principles to computing fields, engage in critical analysis, and undertake independent research endeavors with confidence.

Unit Reflection Pieces

Unit 1: Ethics in Computing in the age of Generative AI

Ethics in Computing in the age of Generative AI poses significant environmental challenges due to the exponential growth of AI research and industry. This reflection highlights the urgent need for cross-border collaboration, renewable resource prioritisation, and government intervention to mitigate environmental footprints. The EU's regulatory framework provides a blueprint for effective governance, but geopolitical tensions and regulatory lag remain significant obstacles. Collaboration among stakeholders and proactive engagement with non-state actors are crucial for driving sustainable AI development.

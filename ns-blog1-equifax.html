The Equifax Data Breach - Firewalls and the Importance of Network Segmentation

The Equifax data breach of 2017 serves as a stark example of the critical role firewalls play in securing sensitive information. In this incident, hackers exploited a vulnerability in Apache Struts, a web application framework. The absence of a robust firewall and inadequate network segmentation allowed the attackers to move laterally within Equifax's network.

Firewall Failures:

Equifax's failure to implement effective firewall measures allowed the attackers to gain unauthorised access to a vast trove of personal and financial data. The breach, affecting nearly 147 million people, highlighted the devastating impact of overlooking fundamental network security measures.

GDPR Implications:

The Equifax breach had severe GDPR implications, as the compromised data included sensitive personal information. The incident underscored the importance of aligning network security practices with data protection regulations to avoid legal and financial repercussions.

Network Segmentation Best Practices:

To mitigate the risk of such breaches, organisations must implement robust firewalls and employ network segmentation. Proper segmentation prevents attackers from easily navigating through the network, limiting the potential damage of a security incident.

Target's Data Breach - Lessons in Secure Network Design

The Target data breach of 2013 stands as a classic example of the importance of secure network design. Hackers gained access to Target's network through a third-party HVAC vendor, exploiting weak network segmentation and inadequate access controls.

Network Design Flaws:

Target's network design allowed lateral movement within the system, enabling the attackers to reach the point of sale registers and compromise credit card information. The incident emphasised the need for organisations to design networks with security in mind from the outset.

Impact on GDPR Compliance:

The fallout from the Target breach included legal consequences and financial losses. Ensuring GDPR compliance requires organisations to adopt a holistic approach to network security, addressing vulnerabilities that could lead to unauthorized access and data breaches.

Best Practices for Secure Networks:

To prevent similar breaches, companies must implement robust network design practices. This includes strict access controls, regular security assessments, and careful consideration of third-party connections to minimize the risk of unauthorised access.
